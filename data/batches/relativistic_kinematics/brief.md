# Batch Brief: Relativistic Kinematics

## Goal

Create the first-wave foundation batch for relativistic kinematics as a topic-centered, source-light slice that supports recognition and routine use before any derivation-heavy expansion.

This batch should give future agents a clean starting point for the canonical relativistic-kinematics neighborhood without collapsing into all of special relativity.

## Target Topic

- `relativistic kinematics`

## Batch Purpose

This batch is meant to capture the minimum defensible structure needed to read and use the relativistic-kinematics material that QFT and HEP-th sources routinely assume.

The batch should support:

- recognizing the core vocabulary in textbook, lecture-note, and paper-facing kinematics passages,
- using the standard relations in routine calculations,
- and keeping the topic distinct from full relativistic dynamics or broader spacetime theory.

## In Scope

Likely high-value topics include:

- inertial frames and events
- spacetime coordinates
- Lorentz transformations
- invariant interval
- proper time
- time dilation
- length contraction
- relativity of simultaneity
- velocity addition
- rapidity
- four-velocity and four-momentum when they are directly used as kinematic tools

## Explicitly Out of Scope

- relativistic dynamics and forces
- curved spacetime and general relativity
- field theory, radiation, or interaction-specific machinery
- derivation-first treatment of the full Lorentz group
- broad special-relativity umbrella nodes unless a concrete decomposition need appears

## Task Model

- `literature_reading`

The batch should stay source-light and topic-centered: enough to support recognition and standard use, but not a derivation-first reconstruction.

## Target Mastery Modes

- `recognize`
- `use`

## Audience/Profile Assumption

- `hep_th_grad`
- subfield focus: `qft_foundations`

## Expected Batch Size

- approximately 6 to 12 nodes
- approximately 8 to 18 dependency edges
- a small number of overlays if a source explicitly treats some kinematic machinery as background

## Review Risks

- drifting from kinematics into dynamics or general relativity
- making the batch too derivation-heavy for a first-wave scaffold
- using broad umbrella nodes where a specific kinematic concept is available
- overfitting to a single textbook presentation instead of a stable topic core

## Deliverable for This Batch

A clean Stage 1 scaffold for relativistic kinematics that future agents can populate with source-backed nodes, dependencies, and overlays while preserving a recognition/use-first interpretation.
