# Source Notes: Effective Field Theory and SCET

These notes are batch-specific extraction context, not canonical graph facts.

## Intended Reading of the Topic

For this batch, `effective field theory and SCET` should be understood in the standard QFT sense:

- EFT as a description organized by scale separation, operator expansion, matching, and power counting,
- HQET as the heavy-quark specialization of that idea,
- and SCET as the soft/collinear specialization used for energetic radiation and factorization-style discussions.

This batch is intended to support paper, lecture-note, and textbook reading in the later-Schwartz setting, not a full derivation-first reconstruction of EFT or factorization proofs.

## What the Batch Is Trying to Capture

This batch should identify the smallest defensible prerequisite slice needed to:

- recognize EFT vocabulary in later chapters,
- use the standard matching and RG language,
- and keep HQET and SCET as distinct reusable branches rather than a single opaque "effective theory" bucket.

The batch is not trying to capture:

- the full renormalization program,
- detailed factorization proofs,
- SMEFT flavor structure,
- or lattice/nonperturbative EFT frameworks.

High-value dependency patterns probably include:

- scale separation into the effective-theory reading
- effective field theory into operator expansion and power counting
- operator expansion into higher-dimensional operators
- matching into RG running
- RG running into large-logarithm resummation
- HQET into heavy-quark symmetry and matching
- SCET into mode scaling, collinear/soft modes, and hard/jet/soft functions
- SCET into large-log resummation when the source is using the standard later-Schwartz language

## Source Guidance

Prioritize later Schwartz EFT and SCET chapters, HQET/SCET lecture notes, and review passages that explicitly define the scale hierarchy and the branch-specific functions or modes.

Prefer a small source set with stable terminology over broad factorization references or proofs that are not needed for the first pass.

## Anti-Patterns

Avoid:

- collapsing the batch into a generic renormalization branch,
- erasing the distinction between HQET and SCET,
- importing factorization proofs before the mode language is stable,
- and treating the later-Schwartz chapter order as ontology.

## Evidence Guidance

Use evidence that shows:

- how authors identify the relevant scales,
- how they introduce matching and power counting,
- where the heavy-quark and soft/collinear specializations split,
- and how hard, jet, and soft functions are used in the SCET reading.

If a candidate feels like broader QCD or renormalization material, flag it instead of forcing it into the first pass.
