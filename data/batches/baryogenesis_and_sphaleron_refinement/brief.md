# Batch Brief: Baryogenesis and Sphaleron Refinement

## Goal

Build a narrow late-SM refinement batch for `baryogenesis`, `sphaleron`, baryon/lepton
number language, and `B - L` symmetry.

This batch should absorb the remaining coherent electroweak-baryogenesis residue without
opening a broad cosmology or BSM umbrella.

## Target Topic

- `late Standard-Model baryogenesis tail`
- `baryon/lepton number and sphaleron language`

## Batch Purpose

This batch is meant to capture the smallest reusable neighborhood that makes the remaining
Schwartz baryogenesis-side vocabulary queryable.

The batch should support:

- reading baryon and lepton number as conserved-charge language descended from Noether
  symmetry,
- recognizing `B - L` as the symmetry combination that remains especially important in
  baryogenesis discussions,
- using `sphaleron` as the electroweak nonperturbative bridge node,
- and treating `Sakharov conditions` as the standard criteria branch under baryogenesis.

## In Scope

Likely high-value topics include:

- baryon number conservation
- lepton number
- B - L symmetry
- baryogenesis
- Sakharov conditions
- sphaleron

## Explicitly Out of Scope

- axion or strong-CP material
- technicolor or broader BSM model-building
- full cosmology or thermal-history ontology

## Overlap Expectations

This batch should explicitly reuse:

- `sm.symmetry_breaking_and_standard_model`
- `classical_fields.noether_theorem`
- `sm.ewbreak.electroweak_symmetry_breaking`

The batch-specific nodes here should capture the remaining baryogenesis and sphaleron
language without opening a generic cosmology namespace by default.

## Review Risks

- turning the batch into a general cosmology branch
- splitting `baryon number` and `baryon number conservation` into redundant nodes
- treating `sphaleron` as detached from electroweak symmetry breaking
- overpromoting `Sakharov conditions` into a standalone cosmology namespace before it is
  warranted elsewhere in the graph

## Deliverable for This Batch

A compact authored refinement that closes the remaining electroweak-baryogenesis vocabulary
tail around charge symmetries, sphalerons, and Sakharov conditions.
