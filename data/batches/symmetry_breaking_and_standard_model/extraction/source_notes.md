# Source Notes: Symmetry Breaking and the Standard Model

These notes are batch-specific extraction context, not canonical graph facts.

## Intended Reading of the Topic

For this batch, `symmetry breaking and the Standard Model` should be understood in the standard electroweak/QFT sense:

- spontaneous symmetry breaking as a property of the vacuum relative to the Lagrangian symmetry,
- the Higgs sector as the mechanism that turns symmetry breaking into particle masses,
- and electroweak symmetry breaking as the route from the gauge-symmetric theory to the observed W, Z, photon, and fermion mass pattern.

This batch is meant to support paper, lecture-note, and textbook reading in the Standard Model setting, not a full gauge-theory derivation or a precision-fit reconstruction.

## What the Batch Is Trying to Capture

This batch should identify the smallest defensible prerequisite slice needed to:

- recognize the symmetry-breaking vocabulary in Standard Model passages,
- follow ordinary Higgs-mechanism explanations,
- and use the electroweak-breaking language that later anomaly and precision branches build on.

The batch is not trying to capture:

- full gauge-fixing or BRST machinery,
- detailed loop corrections,
- anomaly cancellation,
- or BSM Higgs sectors.

## Source Guidance

Prioritize standard SM chapters, Higgs-sector lecture notes, and review passages that explicitly define the vacuum, the Higgs field or doublet, and the symmetry-breaking consequences for masses and gauge bosons.

Prefer a small source set with stable terminology over broad secondary references or highly technical electroweak calculations.

## Anti-Patterns

Avoid:

- collapsing the batch into generic gauge theory,
- jumping straight to precision fits or anomaly cancellation,
- importing renormalization details without direct need,
- and treating the textbook exposition order as if it were the ontology.

## Evidence Guidance

Use evidence that shows:

- how authors define the symmetry-breaking vacuum,
- which field or doublet is used in the Higgs sector,
- how the electroweak masses are described after breaking,
- and where the Standard Model writing still treats the classical-field Lagrangian as background.

If a candidate feels more like later anomaly, precision, or EFT material, flag it instead of forcing it into the first pass.
