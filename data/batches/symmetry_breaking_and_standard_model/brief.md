# Batch Brief: Symmetry Breaking and the Standard Model

## Goal

Build a first-pass reusable neighborhood for spontaneous symmetry breaking, the Higgs sector, and the electroweak basics needed to read Standard Model material in a topic-centered way.

This batch should stay source-light and reuse the classical-field Lagrangian background where helpful, but it should not drift into a full gauge-theory or renormalization reconstruction.

## Target Topic

- `symmetry breaking and the Standard Model`

## Batch Purpose

This batch is meant to capture the minimum defensible structure needed to read the Standard Model around symmetry breaking:

- recognize the symmetry-breaking vocabulary,
- use the Higgs-mechanism and electroweak-breaking language in ordinary reading,
- and keep the neighborhood distinct from later gauge, anomaly, and precision-fit details.

## In Scope

Likely high-value topics include:

- spontaneous symmetry breaking
- vacuum expectation values
- Goldstone bosons in the standard symmetry-breaking reading
- Higgs doublet and Higgs mechanism
- electroweak symmetry and electroweak symmetry breaking
- W and Z bosons, photon, and the electroweak mixing angle
- Yukawa couplings and fermion mass generation
- the classical-field Lagrangian background when it is explicitly used

## Explicitly Out of Scope

- full gauge-theory derivations
- gauge fixing and BRST structure
- detailed renormalization or loop corrections
- anomaly cancellation and precision-test machinery
- BSM Higgs sectors unless a source passage forces them in

- overusing textbook order instead of the actual dependency structure

A clean first-pass Standard Model symmetry-breaking neighborhood that future agents can extend into the anomaly, precision, and EFT branches without losing the reuse-friendly electroweak core.
