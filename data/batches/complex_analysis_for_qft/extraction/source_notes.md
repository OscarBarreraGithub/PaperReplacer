These notes are not canonical graph facts. They are batch-specific extraction context for the autonomous proposer.

## Intended Reading of the Topic

For this batch, `complex analysis for QFT` should be understood in the paper-facing sense:

- the contour and singularity tools that recur in QFT and amplitudes discussions,
- with immediate use cases in reading standard arguments and recognizing familiar analytic moves,
- not as a full derivation program or a complete theory of analytic structure.

## What the Batch Is Trying to Capture

This batch should identify the smallest defensible prerequisite slice needed to:

- recognize the standard complex-analysis language used in QFT papers,
- use the basic contour and singularity ideas in a reading-level setting,
- and avoid jumping directly to deeper derivation machinery before the foundational slice is stable.

The batch is not trying to capture:

- all of QFT,
- a complete analytic-structure ontology,
- or the full derivation of singularity conditions.

## Candidate Node Families

The highest-value node families for the first pass are likely:

- contour deformation
- poles vs branch points
- Feynman `i epsilon` prescription

- recognition-level analytic continuation references when they are directly tied to the above

## Candidate Relation Patterns

Likely high-value dependency patterns include:

- method-level complex-analysis prerequisites into paper-facing QFT uses,
- propagator and prescription structure into contour-trapping arguments,

- and only limited overlay usage for background that advanced readers often assume.

## Anti-Patterns

Avoid:

- broad prerequisite nodes like `QFT` or `complex analysis` unless a sharper decomposition is not available,
- importing Landau conditions, loop-momentum stationarity, or other derivation-first machinery into this first slice,
- treating background familiarity as intrinsic dependency,
- and overfitting the batch to a single derivation when the goal is source-light foundation work.

## Evidence Guidance

When proposing claims, prefer evidence of these forms:

- standard paper-language dependence such as contour deformation and contour-trapping arguments,
- explicit reliance on pole placement from the Feynman prescription,
- distinctions between pole and branch behavior when reading singularity discussion,
- and direct recognition of pinch language in QFT or amplitudes texts.

If a claim feels true only at the level of a broader research program, flag it instead of asserting it for this batch.
