# Source Notes: Discrete Symmetries and CPT

These notes are batch-specific extraction context, not canonical graph facts.

## Intended Reading of the Topic

For this batch, `discrete symmetries and cpt` should be understood as the standard QFT-facing symmetry slice:

- parity, charge conjugation, and time reversal as distinct transformations,
- their action on Dirac spinors and spinor bilinears,
- and the combined CPT statement as it appears in local Lorentz-covariant relativistic field theory.

This batch is intended to support chapter-level reading in the Schwartz family around the discrete-symmetry material, not a proof-first reconstruction of the CPT theorem.

## What the Batch Is Trying to Capture

This batch should identify the smallest defensible prerequisite slice needed to:

- recognize the individual P, C, and T operations when they are named,
- follow how they act on spinors or bilinears,
- and read the combined CPT claim without collapsing it into a generic symmetry slogan.

The batch is not trying to capture:

- the full CPT-theorem proof,
- anomaly machinery,
- gauge-theory symmetry breaking,
- or a complete axiomatization of discrete symmetries in QFT.

## Likely High-Value Node Candidates

The most likely node candidates are:

- parity transformation
- charge conjugation
- time reversal
- CPT theorem
- CPT invariance
- spinor bilinear
- Dirac adjoint

## Likely Edge Patterns

High-value dependency patterns probably include:

- spinor/Dirac background into the discrete-symmetry neighborhood
- Dirac spinors into P, C, and T action language
- gamma matrices into the operator-level descriptions of P, C, and T
- P, C, and T into the CPT theorem
- the CPT theorem into the invariance statement that it supports

## Source Guidance

Prioritize textbook and lecture-note passages that explicitly define the discrete transformations, show how they act on spinor fields or bilinears, and state CPT in the same local relativistic-QFT neighborhood.

Prefer a small source set with stable terminology over broad symmetry treatments that do not stay close to the fermion vocabulary used in QFT.

## Anti-Patterns

Avoid:

- collapsing P, C, and T into one generic symmetry node,
- treating CPT as a stand-alone slogan without the spinor context,
- importing anomaly or gauge-theory machinery too early,
- and forcing a proof-first treatment before the reading-level structure is stable.

## Evidence Guidance

Use evidence that shows:

- how authors distinguish parity, charge conjugation, and time reversal,
- where spinor or bilinear language is used to describe their action,
- and when CPT is presented as a combined theorem rather than as a loose heuristic.

If a candidate feels too close to a later anomaly or symmetry-breaking batch, flag it instead of forcing it into the first pass.
