# Source Notes: Variational Principles and Classical Fields

These notes are batch-specific extraction context, not canonical graph facts.

## Intended Reading of the Topic

For this batch, `variational principles and classical fields` should be understood in the standard classical-field-theory sense:

- fields as spacetime-dependent quantities,
- action-based descriptions built from a Lagrangian density,
- field equations obtained from stationary variations,
- and conserved-quantity language when Noether-style symmetry arguments are directly present.

This batch is intended to support paper, lecture-note, and textbook reading in the QFT foundation setting, not a derivation-first reconstruction of the calculus of variations or a full canonical/Hamiltonian treatment.

## What the Batch Is Trying to Capture

This batch should identify the smallest defensible prerequisite slice needed to:

- recognize the core vocabulary of classical field theory,
- follow the standard action-to-equations-of-motion pipeline,
- and use the common conservation-law vocabulary that appears alongside variational arguments.

The batch is not trying to capture:

- all of classical mechanics,
- canonical or Hamiltonian formalism,
- gauge-specific machinery as a separate branch,
- curved spacetime,
- or a complete axiomatization of variational calculus.

## Likely High-Value Node Candidates

The most likely node candidates are:

- spacetime coordinates, when the source treats fields as functions on spacetime
- field configuration
- Lagrangian density
- action functional
- stationary action principle
- Euler-Lagrange field equation
- boundary terms in variation
- continuous symmetries of the action
- Noether theorem when it is directly tied to the variational setup

## Likely Edge Patterns

High-value dependency patterns probably include:

- spacetime coordinates into field configurations
- field configurations into local Lagrangian densities
- Lagrangian densities into action functionals
- action functionals into the stationary action principle
- action variation into boundary-term handling and then into Euler-Lagrange field equations
- action symmetries into Noether-style conserved-current statements

## Source Guidance

Prioritize introductory field-theory and QFT texts, lecture notes, and review passages that explicitly define the field variables, build the action from a Lagrangian density, and derive the field equations by variation.

Prefer a small source set with stable terminology over broad secondary references or highly formal derivations.

## Anti-Patterns

Avoid:

- collapsing the batch into generic mechanics,
- jumping straight to Hamiltonian or canonical language,
- importing gauge or curved-spacetime machinery without direct evidence,
- and treating a derivation from first principles as mandatory for every node.

## Evidence Guidance

Use evidence that shows:

- how authors define the field variables and spacetime dependence,
- which quantities are treated as the action-building ingredients,
- where boundary terms matter in the variation,
- and when a symmetry statement is explicitly tied to the action rather than presented as a free-standing theorem.

If a candidate feels derivation-heavy, flag it for later expansion instead of forcing it into the first-pass slice.
