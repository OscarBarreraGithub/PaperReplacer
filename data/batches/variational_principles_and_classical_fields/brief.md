# Batch Brief: Variational Principles and Classical Fields

## Goal

Build a Stage 2 population batch for variational principles and classical fields as a topic-centered, source-light slice that supports recognition and ordinary use before any derivation-heavy expansion.

This batch should give future agents a clean, later-QFT-trunk input for the action-based classical-field neighborhood without drifting into canonical mechanics, gauge theory, or full quantization.

## Target Topic

- `variational principles and classical fields`

## Batch Purpose

This batch is meant to capture the minimum defensible structure needed to read and use the classical-field machinery that QFT texts routinely assume.

The batch should support:

- recognizing the core vocabulary of classical field theory,
- using the action principle and the resulting field equations in ordinary reading,
- and keeping the topic distinct from Hamiltonian mechanics or broader field-theory machinery.

## In Scope

Likely high-value topics include:

- fields as spacetime-dependent quantities
- field configurations
- Lagrangian density
- action functional
- stationary action principle
- Euler-Lagrange field equations
- boundary terms in variations
- continuous symmetries of the action
- Noether theorem when it is explicitly tied to the variational setup

## Explicitly Out of Scope

- canonical and Hamiltonian formulations
- gauge-specific machinery unless a concrete source passage forces it
- curved spacetime and general relativity
- full derivation-first treatments of variational calculus
- broad mechanics or QFT umbrellas unless a sharper decomposition is not available

## Task Model

- `literature_reading`

The batch should stay source-light and topic-centered: enough to support recognition and standard use, but not a derivation-first reconstruction.

## Target Mastery Modes

- `recognize`
- `use`

## Audience/Profile Assumption

- `hep_th_grad`
- subfield focus: `qft_foundations`

## Expected Batch Size

- approximately 7 to 12 nodes
- approximately 8 to 16 dependency edges
- a small number of partonomy edges if they clearly help the topic decomposition

## Review Risks

- drifting from action-based classical fields into canonical or Hamiltonian formalism
- overfitting the batch to a single textbook derivation
- using broad umbrella nodes where a local field-theory concept is available
- treating coordinate or symmetry background as if it were universally intrinsic

## Deliverable for This Batch

A clean Stage 2 population slice for variational principles and classical fields that future agents can extend into the QFT trunk while preserving a recognition/use-first interpretation.
