# Batch Brief: Anomalies and Precision Tests in the Standard Model

## Goal

Build a first-pass reusable neighborhood for Standard Model anomalies and electroweak precision tests.

This batch should stay source-light and connect naturally to the symmetry-breaking branch, but it should not balloon into a full renormalization, collider-phenomenology, or BSM anomaly-matching program.

## Target Topic

- `anomalies and precision tests in the Standard Model`

## Batch Purpose

This batch is meant to capture the minimum defensible structure needed to read the Standard Model around:

- anomaly language,
- anomaly cancellation,
- and the main electroweak precision-observable vocabulary.

## In Scope

Likely high-value topics include:

- global, chiral, and gauge anomalies
- anomaly cancellation
- Noether-current background when it is explicitly used
- electroweak precision observables
- oblique corrections and gauge-boson self-energies
- Z-pole observables, W-mass observables, the rho parameter, and the effective weak mixing angle

## Explicitly Out of Scope

- full renormalization-group machinery
- complete electroweak-fits derivations
- collider-specific phenomenology
- flavor-model or BSM anomaly matching
- lattice or nonperturbative anomaly programs

## Task Model

- `literature_reading`

## Target Mastery Modes

- `recognize`
- `use`

## Audience/Profile Assumption

- `hep_th_grad`
- subfield focus: `standard_model`

## Expected Batch Size

- approximately 10 to 14 nodes
- approximately 10 to 16 dependency edges
- a modest branch structure to keep anomaly and precision material separated

## Review Risks

- collapsing anomaly language into generic symmetry talk
- mixing precision-observable language with full renormalization theory
- drifting into later electroweak-fit machinery too early
- treating the Standard Model as a monolithic umbrella instead of separate reusable branches

## Deliverable for This Batch

A clean first-pass anomaly-and-precision neighborhood that can sit beside the symmetry-breaking batch and support later Standard Model and EFT extensions without overcommitting to detailed fit machinery.
