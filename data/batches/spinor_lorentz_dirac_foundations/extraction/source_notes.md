# Source Notes: Spinor, Lorentz, and Dirac Foundations

These notes are batch-specific extraction context, not canonical graph facts.

## Intended Reading of the Topic

For this batch, `spinor lorentz dirac foundations` should be understood as the standard QFT-facing spinor/Dirac slice:

- Lorentz representations that lead to spinor language,
- Dirac and Weyl spinors as representation objects,
- gamma matrices and Dirac algebra,
- chirality language via gamma five and projectors,
- and the Dirac equation as the standard relativistic fermion wave equation.

This batch is intended to support chapter-level reading in the Schwartz chapter family that precedes discrete symmetries, not a full derivation-first treatment of Clifford algebras or fermion quantization.

## What the Batch Is Trying to Capture

This batch should identify the smallest defensible prerequisite slice needed to:

- recognize the spinor vocabulary when it appears in a QFT source,
- read the matrix notation that acts on Dirac or Weyl spinors,
- and use the standard Dirac-equation and chirality language in ordinary text.

The batch is not trying to capture:

- all of group representation theory,
- a full Clifford-algebra derivation program,
- field quantization,
- discrete symmetries as a separate branch,
- or later fermion-interaction machinery.

The most likely node candidates are:

- Lorentz-group representation

## Likely Edge Patterns

High-value dependency patterns probably include:

- Lorentz-representation language into spinor representation language
- spinor representation into Dirac and Weyl spinor vocabulary
- gamma matrices into the Dirac algebra and Dirac equation
- gamma five into chirality projectors
- chirality projectors into Weyl-spinor reading

## Source Guidance

Prioritize textbook and lecture-note passages that explicitly connect the Lorentz-representation story to spinor objects, define the gamma-matrix algebra, and use chirality language in the same neighborhood.

Prefer a small source set with stable terminology over broad representation-theory treatments that do not stay close to the fermion vocabulary used in QFT.

## Anti-Patterns

Avoid:

- collapsing everything into a generic `spinor` umbrella,
- drifting into discrete symmetries before the Dirac notation is stable,
- importing free-field quantization or gauge-theory machinery too early,
- and treating a detailed Clifford-algebra derivation as mandatory for every node.

## Evidence Guidance

Use evidence that shows:

- how authors move from Lorentz representation language into spinor objects,
- where gamma matrices are introduced as the acting notation,
- how gamma five and chirality projectors are used,
- and when the Dirac equation is treated as the standard relativistic fermion relation.

If a candidate feels too close to a later discrete-symmetry or field-quantization batch, flag it instead of forcing it into the first pass.
